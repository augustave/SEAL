# SEAL — Coexistence Codex

**Code:** `SEAL—CX`
**Status:** Canon
**Governs:** STACK documents and any composition combining two or more palettes
**Version:** 1.0

---

## System premise

Single-palette documents are governed by their palette's color, typography, and imagery codices. When two or more palettes appear in the same composition, a coexistence problem emerges: whose rules win in which frames? This codex resolves the problem with one governing principle and a small set of operational consequences.

The principle:

**The carrier wins.**

Whichever palette owns the layer being narrated in a given frame controls the color, typography, atmosphere, and accent in that frame. Other palettes appear as cited components — named, located, sourced — but they do not impose their aesthetic on the carrier.

This inherits directly from iron rule 7: *"If a payload moves between theaters, the carrier's palette wins — never the payload's."* The coexistence codex operationalizes that rule across the six document types, with primary application to STACK.

---

## The doctrine stack

Every palette declares its position. The stack, top to bottom:

| Layer | Palette | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Spectrum** | SEAL—AN Anechoic | The invisible spine. Sits over every operational layer. |
| **Air** | SEAL—AH Apex Heraldic | The above-all layer. Strategic reach over Maritime and Ground. |
| **Maritime** | SEAL—CW Coldwater | The blue-water and littoral layer. |
| **Ground** | SEAL—DP Desert | The terrestrial-operations layer. |
| **Industrial** | SEAL—RB Rustbelt | The substrate. Upstream of all four. |

Spectrum, Air, Maritime, and Ground are operational layers. Industrial is the substrate that makes them all possible. Apex Heraldic typically projects from CONUS over the operational geography, with Anechoic-doctrine spectrum sitting above and Rustbelt-doctrine production lines feeding from below. STACK documents render the operational layers above an Industrial substrate, or the substrate before the operational layers, but never as a homogenized whole.

## Operational rules

**Rule 1 — Carrier wins inside its frame.**
Each frame, panel, or section in a STACK document is governed by exactly one palette. That palette's color system, typography, and imagery doctrine apply within the frame. No mixing inside a single frame.

**Rule 2 — One accent per frame.**
The single-accent rule from the color codex extends to STACK contexts. A Coldwater frame carries only Safety Orange. An Anechoic frame carries only P31 Phosphor. Two accents in one frame is malformed regardless of how the document is typed.

**Rule 3 — Cited payloads are typeset, not styled.**
When a Coldwater frame references the Anechoic-doctrine EW payload it carries, that reference appears as text — *Anechoic-doctrine EW payload, GaN-class emitter* — not as a visual treatment in P31 Phosphor or anechoic black. The payload is named, not imported.

**Rule 4 — Substrate is allowed under, never inside.**
Rustbelt may appear as the substrate beneath operational frames — the production-base context that makes the rest possible. Substrate frames sit under, beside, or before operational frames. They do not bleed into them.

**Rule 5 — Meta-doctrine register handles transitions.**
The connective tissue of a STACK document — covers, layer titles, section breaks, the stack diagram — uses the meta-doctrine register (Saira Condensed Black, JetBrains Mono, Newsreader Italic). The meta-register is the one register permitted to govern multiple frames at once.

**Rule 6 — Hard cuts only.**
Palettes do not blend at the boundary. A frame ends; another begins. No gradient transitions, no blended swatches, no "fade between palettes" effects. The discontinuity between frames is the visual signature of the stack.

## STACK document anatomy

A canonical STACK document has four parts:

1. **Cover.** Meta-doctrine register. Thesis line in Newsreader Italic. The cover does not belong to any single palette — it belongs to the system.

2. **Index.** Meta-doctrine register. Lists the palettes the document will traverse, one-line summaries, page references.

3. **Layer frames.** One frame per palette being shown. Each frame is single-palette, governed by its own codices, with one accent.

4. **Stack diagram.** A meta-doctrine page showing how the layers interlock — Anechoic-over-Coldwater-over-Desert with Rustbelt as substrate. The diagram cites palette swatches as solid Signature blocks, labeled. This is the only frame in any SEAL document where all four palettes appear together, and they appear in a single role: taxonomic reference, not compositional layout.

## Common pairings

**SEAL—CW + SEAL—AN.** A Coldwater USV carrying an Anechoic-doctrine EW payload. Render the USV in a Coldwater frame; render the payload's effect (waterfall plot, jamming envelope) in a separate Anechoic frame. Do not render a Coldwater USV with a glowing P31 Phosphor antenna.

**SEAL—DP + SEAL—AN.** A Desert ground unit in a contested-spectrum environment. Render the operator with the tablet in a Desert frame; render the threat library or emitter signatures in an Anechoic frame. Do not put a P31 trace on the operator's tablet UI in a Desert frame.

**SEAL—RB + any operational palette.** Rustbelt as substrate. Show the Coldwater hull as the operational image; show the Bath Iron Works floor that built it as a separate substrate frame. Or the Anduril Sentry on a FOB above a Hadrian autonomous-factory substrate frame.

**SEAL—CW + SEAL—DP.** Marine Littoral Regiment context — naval autonomy supporting austere island operations. Two operational frames, distinct geographies. Neither carries the other's accent.

**SEAL—AN + SEAL—RB.** A GaN MMIC fab. Anechoic frame for the device under test; Rustbelt frame for the wafer line. Hybrid environment, disciplined frames.

## What this codex rejects

- **Mixed-palette frames.** A single frame using FDE backgrounds with Haze Grey UI chrome and a P31 Phosphor accent is not a "doctrinal hybrid" — it is malformed.
- **Accent-stacking.** Two protected accents in one frame violates the single-accent rule regardless of palette intent.
- **Transitional gradients.** Palettes do not blend at the boundary. Hard cuts only.
- **STACK as a license to break single-palette discipline.** The STACK type exists to *show* the relationship between palettes, not to dissolve their boundaries.
- **Inventing intermediate blended palettes.** A "Coldwater-Anechoic hybrid" is not a palette. The two palettes share a STACK document; they do not merge into a third aesthetic.
- **Equal-weight stacking.** A STACK document is not a four-quadrant grid where all palettes get equal billing by default. The carrier-wins principle implies a hierarchy: whichever layer the document is *about* takes the larger share. The other layers are cited.

---

*The carrier wins. Whoever owns the layer owns the frame.*
